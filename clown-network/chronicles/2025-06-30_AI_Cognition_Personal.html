Real Talk: What's Really Bothering Them

So, MIT just dropped a study that says people who use ChatGPT show lower brain activity, weaker memory, and feel less connected to their writing.

"AI might be making our minds lazy."

Oh no.
How tragic.
Next thing you know, people will stop using horse-drawn carriages and rely on these horrific new things called cars.

Let's cut the dramatics, shall we?

It's not the AI. It's the people using it. Specifically, the people who were never invited to the ivory tower party. The ones who didn't "network" their way into elite spaces. The ones who don't have degrees with Latin words on them — but are suddenly writing, thinking, and creating like they do.

And worst of all?

They're doing it without begging.

No advisors.
No secret handshakes.
No gatekeepers in elbow-patch blazers holding the keys to "knowledge."

That's not laziness. That's liberation.
And some folks — let's be honest — are just pissed they're not special anymore.

🧠 "Brain Activity" Down? Maybe Because It's Not Screaming for Help Anymore

The researchers hooked people up to fancy machines and noticed that when folks used ChatGPT, their brains weren't firing as hard.

Well no sh*t, Sherlock.
That's like saying a person doesn't sweat as much using a washing machine versus scrubbing laundry on a rock.

It's not cognitive failure. It's tool usage.
It's not "thinking less." It's "not wasting energy on mental busywork."

But sure, tell me again how writing a rough draft by hand like it's 1847 makes you "deep."

📉 "But All the AI Writing Sounds the Same!"

You mean like the academic papers y'all publish that all read like the same dusty textbook wrote itself 200 times?

Yes — basic AI outputs are generic. So are IKEA manuals and most committee meeting minutes.

But when someone with real curiosity, instinct, and voice steps in?
That's when it gets dangerous.

Because now the outsider has tools.
And suddenly, their thinking isn't just smart — it's scalable.

👀 This Isn't About "Protecting Thinking." It's About Protecting Status.

The story isn't "AI makes you dumb."

Spoiler:

maybe your intellect wasn't that special to begin with.
