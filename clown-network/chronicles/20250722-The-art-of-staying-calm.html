The breaking point came on what seemed like an ordinary morning. I was getting ready for the day when my roommate approached me, her tone gentle but pointed.

"You left the corridor light on all night," she said. "It attracted mosquitoes into my room."

Here's the thing: I hadn't left the light on. I was certain of this. But as I stood there, I realized I had two choices.

Option 1: Defend myself. Explain that I hadn't touched that light switch. Point out that mosquitoes don't actually need light to find their way into rooms. Risk escalating a situation that was already tense.

Option 2: Respond with grace.

I chose option 2. I smiled genuinely, wished her a good morning, and continued preparing for my day. No argument. No defense. No drama.

In that moment, I learned something powerful: sometimes the strongest response is no response at all. Not because the other person is right, but because preserving peace is more valuable than being right.
